Abiogenesis

DESIGN Process

Greyboxing

Greyboxing started with simple shapes and test assets to display the cave structure we wanted to add to our game. The idea was to make something obvious enough for the player to want to explore and use that curiosity as a way to point them in the right direction.

Using Assets

Using the asset store for a better representation of my vision. I used free assets from the Unity Asset Store to place props and add color to the level to experiment with how I could create a desert. I didn’t want to jump straight to making an alien planet because I needed to see what a desert would look like before altering it.

Desired Colors

The game of reference for color was Astroneer. Using a mixture of vibrant colors but muting them to create that desert feel was the plan. I wanted to create the feeling that the environment use to be and feel alive but then died off over time, creating a dry and barren environment.

Particle Effects and Colors

I added the colors for testing purposes and added the particle effects to see if they would fit with the game. I had the idea of the player being in a desert storm at the start of the game. But, it was later taken out due to performance issues during playtesting.

The Final Product

The final product of the level became exactly what I envisioned it to be. Dull in the beginning, a dark cave that uses crystals and mushrooms to illuminate the environment, and a very vibrant and alive environment that the player would travel through.
